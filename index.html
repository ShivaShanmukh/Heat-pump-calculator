🌡️ Indoor Temperature (Undersized Scenario)

About the Indoor Temperature Calculation (Part 3)

  • When the heat pump can't deliver the full heating load, the indoor temperature drops below the setpoint.
  • The actual indoor temperature is estimated as: T_indoor = T_outdoor + (Q_delivered × 1000) / UA, where UA is the total thermal transmittance.
  • Limitations: This is a steady-state model. A more accurate simulation would need:
    • Thermal mass of the building (brick, concrete, etc.) to model heat storage
    • Ventilation losses (air changes per hour × volume)
    • Solar gains through windows
    • Internal gains from occupants and appliances
  • With thermal mass data, a dynamic model could show a gradual temperature decay rather than the instantaneous drop shown here.
